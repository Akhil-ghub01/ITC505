A Mathematical Theory of Communication

Claude Shannon

Abstract

This paper introduces the fundamental principles of information theory, establishing the mathematical framework for communication systems.

Introduction

Claude Shannon’s work forms the basis of modern digital communication, defining key concepts such as entropy, redundancy, and channel capacity.

Mathematical Foundations

The paper describes the mathematical representation of information, including the entropy formula and probability-based transmission models.

Communication Channels

Shannon details how different types of communication channels affect the transmission of information, including the impact of noise.

Applications

The theory has applications in coding, cryptography, and modern telecommunications.

Conclusion

Shannon’s theory laid the groundwork for advancements in data compression, error detection, and digital signal processing.

Bibliography
